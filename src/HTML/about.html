What are AMPs?

Antimicrobial peptides (AMPs) are peptides that form part of the innate immune response in all major groups of organisms. AMPs are potent antibiotics that have demonstrated the ability to kill a broad spectrum of bacteria, viruses and fungi, with some even having the ability to kill cancerous cells. AMPs thus can serve as potential novel therapeutic agents.
